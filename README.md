What is the Full Form of UPSC?

The full form of UPSC is Union Public Service Commission.

It is a central organization in India that is responsible for conducting various prestigious government examinations and selecting candidates for top civil services posts.

What Does UPSC Do?

The main role of UPSC is to recruit candidates for government jobs through competitive exams. It ensures a fair, transparent, and merit-based selection process.

Some major exams conducted by UPSC include:

Civil Services Examination (IAS, IPS, IFS)
Indian Forest Service Examination
Combined Defence Services (CDS)
National Defence Academy (NDA)
Engineering Services Examination
Why is UPSC Important?

UPSC plays a key role in building the administration of the country. Officers selected through UPSC exams work in important positions and help in decision-making, law enforcement, foreign services, and policy formation.

This makes UPSC one of the most respected examination bodies in India.

Who Can Apply for UPSC Exams?

To apply for UPSC exams, candidates generally must:

Be a graduate from a recognized university
Meet the age criteria (usually 21 to 32 years for general category)
Fulfill other eligibility conditions depending on the exam
